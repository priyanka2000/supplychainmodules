Document Scope
This document covers the complete engineering architecture and implementation plan to evolve the SYDIAI Supply Chain Intelligence Suite from a prototype into an enterprise-grade, demo-ready planning platform for the beverage industry. It includes improvement themes, a 60+ task engineering backlog, module-wise breakdown, UI/UX tasks, data architecture, a phased roadmap, and beverage-specific demo scenarios.

Section 1
Major Improvement Themes
Ten critical areas to evolve from prototype to enterprise-grade platform
01
Supply Chain Network Modeling
Enterprise SC platforms require a structured graph model of nodes (plants, DCs, markets, suppliers) and edges (lanes, flows). Without it, planners cannot visualise end-to-end impact of decisions. Critical for multi-echelon optimisation and disruption simulation.
02
Optimization Engine Implementation
Rule-based heuristics are insufficient for enterprise planning. MIP/LP solvers are required for production scheduling, deployment routing, and inventory positioning. Best-in-class beverage companies use mixed-integer programming for daily planning cycles.
03
Scenario Simulation Engine
Planners need to evaluate "what-if" situations before committing to a plan. Summer demand surges, plant shutdowns, and promotional spikes must be simulatable in <30 seconds with full KPI impact visibility across all downstream modules.
04
Supply Chain Control Tower
A unified real-time command centre that surfaces exceptions across all modules, shows network node health, and enables one-click resolution. The control tower is the primary interface for SC Directors and operations leads.
05
Planner Workflow Redesign
Current module-centric navigation forces planners to context-switch. An integrated planner workbench that surfaces priority decisions, pending approvals, and cross-module actions in a single view dramatically improves planner productivity.
06
Exception Management System
Reactive firefighting must be replaced with proactive exception detection. A severity-ranked exception queue with root-cause analysis, recommended actions, and one-click module navigation is essential for an enterprise-ready system.
07
AI / Data Science Layer
Statistical demand forecasting (Holt-Winters, XGBoost), stockout risk prediction, demand sensing, and OTD risk scoring are standard in leading beverage company SC suites. The AI layer transforms the platform from a reporting tool into a decision-support engine.
08
Visualization Improvements
Enterprise demos require P10/P50/P90 forecast fan charts, Gantt planners, network topology maps, benchmark comparisons, and drill-down KPI cards. Data-rich, context-aware visualisations are critical for client credibility.
09
Data Integration Architecture
A production-ready platform requires defined master data models, demand data ingestion pipelines, inventory integration, transportation cost models, and a planning data lake. Without this, the system cannot scale beyond demo data.
10
Multi-User Collaboration & Security
Role-based access control (SC Director vs Planner vs Analyst), plan approval workflows, audit trails, and collaborative annotation are mandatory for enterprise deployment. Single-user prototypes cannot be used in production environments.

  • Attributes: cost/case, transit days, OTD %, SLA

  • Historical performance: OTIF, delay patterns

5.2 Demand Data Ingestion Pipeline

Pipeline Architecture
Stage 1 — Ingestion: ERP sales history → Blob storage (raw CSV/JSON) → D1 staging table
Stage 2 — Cleansing: Outlier detection, promo tagging, event calendar alignment, lag correction
Stage 3 — Modelling: Holt-Winters statistical baseline + XGBoost driver model → P10/P50/P90
Stage 4 — Sensing: Real-time signal fusion (weather, sell-out, POS) → short-horizon adjustment
Stage 5 — Output: Weekly forecast by SKU × DC → Planning DB → S&OP review

Section 7
Demo Use Cases — Beverage Industry
End-to-end planning decision narratives for client presentations
🌡️ Demo 1 — Summer Demand Surge (+25%)
Trigger: Temperature forecast +4°C above seasonal average across West & South India in April. AI sensing model detects +25% demand uplift for PET 500ml, Mango 200ml, SportZ Energy.
  • Control Tower flags demand uplift signal — Demand Intelligence shows P90 band breached
  • Scenario Lab: run "Summer Surge +25%" — MIP solver calculates production gap in 1.8s
  • Gap: 118,000 cases. MUM at 97% util — headroom only 2,700 cases
  • System recommends: DEL overtime (+8K), CHN 3rd shift (+6K), pre-build 18K cases in W4-Mar
  • S&OP Intelligence Board: 4 gap resolution options with cost/risk shown to board
  • SC Director approves Mumbai overtime → MPS updated → MRP triggered → POs raised
  • Result: 0 stockouts, revenue protected, cost ₹18.4L, net benefit ₹2.8Cr
🏭 Demo 2 — Mumbai Plant Partial Shutdown
Trigger: Boiler inspection + L2 line overhaul required. MUM-L2 offline 10 days (Apr W1–W2). Production loss: 22,400 cases.
  • Exception appears in Control Tower: "MUM-L2 Capacity Breach — W1-W2"
  • Scenario Lab: run "Plant Disruption — Mumbai L2". Solver identifies 3 reallocation options
  • Reallocation plan: DEL-L1 takes 9,600 cases, CHN-L1 takes 7,200, BAN-L1 takes 5,600
  • Deployment workbench: new routes configured for inter-plant supply lanes
  • MRP explosion: additional RM procurement triggered for DEL and CHN plants
  • Pre-build 18K cases in W4-Mar from WH-MUM buffer stock (8.4 days cover)
  • Result: 99.6% demand coverage, 0 customer stockouts, ₹6.8L incremental cost
🥭 Demo 3 — Mango Promotion Spike
Trigger: Marketing approves national Mango 200ml promo for April 15–30. Demand uplift forecast: +34% over 2 weeks. Current Mango production capacity: CHN-L1 only.
  • Demand Intelligence: AI model detects Mango demand band widening — P90: +72K cases
  • Scenario Lab: "Mango Promo — April" simulation. Shows CHN-L1 bottleneck in W3
  • RCCP: CHN-L1 load reaches 98% in promo weeks. BAN-L1 has 28% headroom
  • Campaign scheduler: allocate Mango campaign to BAN-L1 W2-W3 to share load
  • MRP explosion: additional concentrate procurement raised — PO-2024-0892 approved
  • Inventory: pre-position 24K cases at WH-CHN and WH-BAN before promo start
  • Result: Promo OTD 96.4%, fill rate 98.1%, no capacity breach, ₹4.8Cr revenue uplift
📦 Demo 4 — Network Inventory Rebalancing
Trigger: Delhi DC at 91% fill rate (near-full). Mumbai DC at 78% (optimal). Hyderabad DC at 83% (near-full). North market demand declining. South market demand rising.
  • Control Tower: WH-DEL flagged critical (91% fill) — incoming 48T truck Mar 19
  • Network Map: optimizer identifies 3 inter-DC transfer opportunities
  • Run Network LP: move 12,000 cases Glass 500ml WH-DEL → WH-HYD (saves ₹1.2L/mo)
  • Deployment workbench: inter-DC transfer shipment created, carrier assigned
  • Safety stock recalculated using MEIO model: reduce DEL SS by 8K cases
  • Inventory scenarios: ABC-XYZ policy updated for North region slow-movers
  • Result: WH-DEL fill drops to 76%, stockout risk eliminated, ₹3.4L carrying cost saved
🆕 Demo 5 — New SKU Launch (SportZ Energy)
Trigger: New SKU "SportZ Energy 250ml" launching May 1. Forecast: 12,600 cases/week ramp-up. No production history. Requires new BOM, line qualification, and distribution setup.
  • Pack-size master updated: SportZ 250ml added with cases/pallet, truck capacity
  • BOM created: new flavour concentrate, can format, packaging components
  • MRP explosion: gross/net requirements for 8-week ramp-up calculated
  • Production scenarios: line qualification BAN-L1 — RCCP shows 4% headroom at peak
  • Demand Intelligence: new SKU added with P10/P50/P90 bands, accuracy TBD
  • Deployment: initial allocation West + South markets, 5 DCs in Phase 1
  • ATP confirmed for launch week: 14,200 cases available, commit date May 1
🚛 Demo 6 — Carrier Disruption (VRL Logistics)
Trigger: VRL Logistics OTIF drops to 85.6% vs 88% SLA. Delhi→Lucknow lane OTD at 82.4%. 3 delayed shipments in rolling 30 days. Risk of SLA breach penalty.
  • Exception in Control Tower: "Carrier VRL OTIF below SLA" — severity: medium
  • Deployment carriers: VRL scorecard shows declining trend over 6 weeks
  • Scenario Lab: "Carrier Disruption — VRL" — model estimates OTD impact -6.8%
  • Network optimizer: switch Delhi→Lucknow to BlueDart — +8.4% OTD, -4.2% cost
  • Procurement: performance notice issued to VRL, backup carrier qualified
  • SLA tracker: corrective action plan sent, 30-day improvement window set
  • Result: Lane OTD recovers to 93.4% in 2 weeks, ₹0 penalty incurred
